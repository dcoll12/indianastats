Methodology

The IN-Index measures partisan lean for each Indiana legislative and congressional district. For Senate districts it is the average of all available actual race margins plus the 2024 presidential margin (Indiana Senate is staggered: even-cycle SDs ran in 2020 & 2024; odd-cycle SDs ran in 2022). For Congressional districts it averages actual 2020/2022/2024 race margins plus the 2024 presidential. For House districts it equals the 2024 presidential margin; for unopposed 2024 seats, available 2020/2022 race results are also averaged in.

'24 Pres uses precinct-level results aggregated directly by district — the most accurate method available. Race shows the most recent contested race for that district.

Senate '20/'22 columns show the actual State Senate race result for the year that district ran. N/A means no election in that year (staggered cycle). Unop. = uncontested.

House '20/'22 columns show the actual State House race result for each district. Congressional '22 shows the actual 2022 US House race result. The two-party margin is (R votes − D votes) / (R votes + D votes). A result of "+10R" means the district leans Republican by 10 percentage points.

Data Sources: Election results: Indiana Secretary of State — indianavoters.in.gov/ENRHistorical/ElectionResults. District boundaries and representatives: IndianaMap (indianamap.org).
