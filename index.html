Methodology

The IN-Index measures partisan lean for each Indiana legislative and congressional district. For Congressional and Senate districts it is the average of 2020 presidential, 2022 US Senate, and 2024 presidential margins. For House districts it equals the 2024 presidential margin — the only district-specific presidential measure available for all 100 seats.

2024 data uses precinct-level results from the VEST (Voting and Election Science Team) Indiana 2024 general election dataset. Precinct votes are aggregated directly by district using the district assignments in the shapefile attribute table — the most accurate method available.

2022 data uses county-level US Senate results for Congressional and Senate districts (apportioned by census block weights). For House districts, the 2022 column shows the actual 2022 State House race result for each district.

2020 data uses county-level presidential results for Congressional and Senate districts (apportioned by census block weights). For House districts, the 2020 column shows the actual 2020 State House race result. The two-party margin is (R votes − D votes) / (R votes + D votes). A result of "+10R" means the district leans Republican by 10 percentage points.

Data Sources: 2024 precinct results: VEST Indiana 2024 (Harvard Dataverse). 2022 US Senate county results: MIT Election Data and Science Lab / MEDSL (senate_2022.csv). 2020 county results: tonmcg/US_County_Level_Election_Results_08-24. Block assignments: PlanScore.org. District boundaries and representatives: Indiana Map (maps.indiana.edu).
