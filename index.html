About the IN-Index

The IN-Index (Indiana Partisan Lean Index) shown next to each district measures how far that district leans Republican or Democratic. The two-party margin formula is (R votes − D votes) / (R votes + D votes) — a positive result means Republican lean (e.g., "+10R"), negative means Democratic lean (e.g., "+6D").

Congressional districts average the 2020 presidential margin, the 2022 US House race result, and the 2024 presidential margin (three inputs). State Senate districts average the available actual Senate race margin (2020 or 2022, depending on the staggered cycle) with the 2024 presidential margin. State House districts use the 2024 presidential margin; for unopposed 2024 seats, available 2020/2022 State House race results are also averaged in.

Data Sources: Election results: Indiana Secretary of State — indianavoters.in.gov/ENRHistorical/ElectionResults. District boundaries: IndianaMap (indianamap.org). Full methodology: IN Partisan Lean Index page.
